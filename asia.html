Forest Owlet

A rather small bird, different to its owl cousins it usually hunts during the day for prey that is sometimes twice as big as itself with its abnormally large claws. Once thought to be extinct, over a century would pass until the Forest Owlet was rediscovered in 1997 in Maharashtra, India. Since then many more owlets were found, but mostly in pairs all over the place rather than one congregated area. This fragmentation of where the owlets are living most likely stems from illegal logging and the natural growth of agriculture as time goes on. Today, there is an estimated population of between 70-400 in the world today.
