Europe
Northern Bald Ibis
Once a wide-spread species from Africa to Asia, now there are only 200 that exist in the world, most if not all in Morocco and Turkey now. Being around 3 feet tall and relatively heavy, they mostly feed on insects, fruit, and small mammals/reptiles. Due to habitat destruction and mass use of pesticides, their population in the wild has been greatly decreasing.
Dalmatian Pelican
One of the largest birds in the world, the Dalmatian Pelican generally live in Southern Europe that mainly hunts fish via using the pouch underneath their beaks to scoop fish out of the water rather than just stabbing or biting them. Once having a population in the millions across Europe and Asia, they were reduced to the thousands in the 1800s due to the use of pesticides, overfishing, and illegal hunting. Since then, the population has increased to around 10,000 due to conservation efforts.
